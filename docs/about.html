The Editorial Method

How AI Press Review is built

40 sources. 15 minutes. What matters in AI today. — Here is exactly how we select, weight, and structure every episode.

01

Who this is for

AI Press Review is built for professionals whose work is affected by AI — but who don't have time to monitor 40 sources themselves. Finance, banking, legal, healthcare, consulting, and enterprise technology are the primary domains represented in our editorial weighting.

Each episode is designed for a morning commute or a focused 15-minute break. You don't need a technical background. You need to know what changed, what it means for your sector, and what signal is worth tracking next week.

How episodes are structured

Each daily episode follows the same six-pillar structure. Pillars are not always equal in length — the day's material determines the balance — but all six are checked against available sources before each episode is finalized.

📡
AI News
Verified announcements from labs, companies, and institutions
🔬
Research & Breakthroughs
Peer-reviewed publications and lab technical releases
⚙️
Use Cases
Documented enterprise and sector deployments
🛠
Tools & Practice
Practitioner-relevant releases, APIs, and frameworks
📶
Weak Signals
Early patterns not yet in mainstream coverage
🎓
Education
Concepts and context for non-technical audiences

Weekly recap (Saturdays)

Every Saturday episode steps back from daily news to identify the five trends that defined the week — connecting dots across the daily episodes and flagging what will likely matter most in the following week.

03

How sources are selected and weighted

Every episode draws from 40+ global sources gathered in the 12 hours preceding publication. Sources are not treated equally. Each carries a weight score based on editorial standards, domain authority, and primary-source proximity. The higher the weight, the more a source's content influences the episode structure and emphasis.

Tier Weight Examples Rationale
Primary ×3 Lab blogs, official company pages, preprint repositories Official announcements, peer-reviewed research — highest source authority
Press ×2 Major wire services, financial press, academic journals Editorial standards, original reporting, verified by news desk
Specialist Press & Tech ×1 Tech news outlets, industry publications Industry coverage — used for context and signal, not primary sourcing

Source tiers are applied automatically during episode production.

04

What we exclude, and why

Editorial discipline is as much about what you leave out as what you include. The following categories are systematically excluded from every episode, regardless of how widely they circulate on a given day.

Regulatory speculation
Unverified regulatory moves produce noise that is almost always revised. We wait for official filings.
Uncorroborated claims
If only one source carries a claim with no independent corroboration, it doesn't air.
Opinion & commentary
We report what happened, not what commentators think about it. Interpretation is yours.
Small funding rounds
Rounds below $50M and pre-seed announcements crowd out operational signal.
Engagement-driven content
Social media posts, thought leadership, and content farms are excluded regardless of virality.
