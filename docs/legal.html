3 · AI content generation disclosure

AI Press Review operates an automated editorial pipeline that makes extensive use of artificial intelligence at multiple stages of content production. The following disclosures are made in the interest of full transparency.

Script generation by large language models

Episode scripts are generated using large language models (LLMs), which may include models provided by Anthropic, Google, Mistral AI, and other third-party providers, depending on availability. LLMs are probabilistic systems that synthesise text based on training data and input context. They are capable of producing output that is:

  • Factually incorrect (hallucination) — LLMs may generate plausible-sounding statements that are not supported by, or are inconsistent with, the source material provided to them.
  • Subtly distorted — Summarisation may introduce emphasis, omission, or framing that does not fully reflect the nuance of the original source.
  • Outdated — Model training data has a knowledge cutoff; even when provided with current source material, the model's reasoning may be shaped by prior training.
  • Inconsistent — The same input may produce materially different outputs across generation runs due to the stochastic nature of LLM inference.

The human editor reviews generated scripts prior to publication. However, this review does not constitute an exhaustive fact-check against every primary source cited. Errors may pass through the review process.

AI-generated audio (text-to-speech)

Episode audio is produced by an AI text-to-speech system (Cartesia). Synthesised speech may affect the perceived meaning of content through unintended intonation, emphasis, mispronunciation of proper nouns, or pacing artefacts. The audio recording should not be treated as more authoritative than the written transcript, where one is available.

Automated source scoring and filtering

Articles are scored and filtered by an algorithm that assigns weights based on domain authority, keyword relevance, signal terms, and recency. This scoring system was designed by the editor but operates autonomously at runtime. It may:

  • Exclude relevant stories that do not match scoring criteria.
  • Include low-quality or inaccurate articles that match surface-level signals.
  • Amplify high-authority sources even when their reporting contains errors.
  • Apply deduplication logic that removes articles that differ meaningfully from near-duplicates.

AI Press Review content is a processed derivative of its source corpus. It is not a substitute for direct engagement with primary sources, and it should not be cited as an independent journalistic source.

No representation regarding third-party AI models

AI Press Review makes no representation as to the accuracy, safety, or fitness for purpose of any third-party AI model referenced in episode content. Coverage of model capabilities, benchmark results, and safety evaluations reflects publicly reported information at the time of recording and does not constitute an independent technical assessment.
